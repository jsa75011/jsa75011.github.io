Black Panther Party CO-Founders

Bobby Seale

Bobby Seale was born on October, 22 1936 in Dallas, Texas. As a child, his family moved to Oakland, California, where he attended Berkeley High School. In 1955, he joined the U.S Air Force, but after a serious altercation with a superior officer, he was discharged on the grounds of bad conduct. In 1962, Seale enrolled into Oakland’s Merit College, where he met Huey P. Newton at a protest rally to speak out against the blockade of Cuba which was issued under the Kennedy Administration. The two became close friends quickly, and bonded over their political radicalism.

In 1966, the two founded the Black Panther Party, and wrote the “Ten-Point Program” to address educational, economic and social issues facing the black community. During his fight for justice, Seale wrote a book entitled “Seize the Time: The Story of the Black Panther Party and Huey P. Newton”, which was published in 1970. He was arrested numerous times, and was indicted in 1969, in Chicago, for the conspiracy to incite riots. Then, again in 1969, he was charged with murder, but the charges were eventually dropped. He continues to fight for change and remains an advocated for black rights and social justice.

Black Panther Party CO-Founders

Huey P. Newton

Huey P. Newton, originally named Huey Pearce Newton, was born in Monroe, Louisiana on February 17, 1942. Newton’s father found great admiration in Louisiana’s governor at the time, Huey P. Long, and decided to name Newton after him. Newton found himself immersed in political activism early on, because his mother and father were both heavily involved in the NAACP(National Associatin for the Advancement of Colore People). As a toddler, his family moved to Oakland, California. Newton later attended Oakland’s Merritt College, where he met Bobby Seale. Together they created the Black Panther Party for Self Defense in 1966.

As a group, the Black Panther Party had a militaristic undertone, and actively engaged in patrols on the California streets, to monitor police brutally, in which they openly carried fire arms, and wore black leather jackets and black berets. In 1974, Newton fled to Cuba to avoid a murder trial, which was eventually dismissed after two years. Amidst the numerous allegations placed against him, Newton managed to return to the U.S and earned a Ph.D. in 1980, from the University of California, Santa Cruz in social philosophy. Then, on August 22, 1989, Newton was fatally shot in Oakland, California, ended one of the most powerful eras in Black American history.

A Brief Black Panther Party Time Line

  • 1966: The BPP (Black Panther Party) is founded by Huey P. Newton and Bobby Seale
  • 1967: The first BPP office is opened in Oakland, California
  • 1967: Kathleen Cleaver joins the BPP
  • 1968: Huey P. Newton is given a 2 to 15 year jail sentence
  • 1968: FBI declares the BPP to be a major threat to the country
  • 1969: The Free Breakfast for Children Program is opened in Oakland, California
  • 1969: Seale is convicted to 4 years in prison
  • 1970: Seale publishes “Seize the Time: The Story of the Black Panther Party and Huey P. Newton”
  • mid 1970s: The party is severely split and neither Seale nor Cleaver are apart of the group
  • 1977: Huey P. Newton returns to Oakland from Cuba
  • 1982: BPP ends operations
  • 1989: Huey P. Newton is killed in Oakland, California
